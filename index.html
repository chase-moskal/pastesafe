pastesafe might be handy if you need somebody to send you a password or something like that

pastesafe works best in google chrome, and doesn't work on some phones

all pastesafe communications are initiated by an invite link

  1. bob wants to receive secret info from alice. so, bob creates a pastesafe session and sends her the invite link
  2. alice clicks the invite link, and is prompted to enter her secret info for bob
  3. pastesafe packs the secret response into an encrypted link, which only bob can decode
  4. alice sends back this new response link, and bob clicks to decode it

about pastesafe

  • 100% open source on github (isc licensed)
  • 100% serverless clientside webcrypto, keys in localstorage
  • warning pastesafe is experimental pre-release technology. use at your own risk

crypto details

  • each session on your pastesafe dashboard represents an rsa keypair you have loaded in your browser's localstorage. if you delete them, they're gone forever. don't clear your cookies because you'll loose your sessions permanently
  • the invite link contains the session-id and the public-key
  • responses are encrypted with aes-gcm-256, the key for that is encrypted with the rsa public key
  • if you have a copy of the session in your localstorage, you can decrypt the message
